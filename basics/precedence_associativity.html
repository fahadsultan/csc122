Implicit Type Conversion#

Automatic conversion from one data type to another data type as result of an operation.

Python always converts “smaller” data types to “larger” data types to avoid the loss of information.

integer_part = 21
fractional_part = 0.03

print("Datatype of integer_part:", type(integer_part))
print("Datatype of fractional_part:", type(fractional_part))

new_number = integer_part + fractional_part

print("Value of new_number:",new_number)
print("Datatype of new_number:",type(new_number))

print("Datatype of integer_part:", type(integer_part))

Datatype of integer_part: <class 'int'>
Datatype of fractional_part: <class 'float'>
Value of new_number: 21.03
Datatype of new_number: <class 'float'>
Datatype of integer_part: <class 'int'>

integer_part = "21"
fractional_part = "0.03"

new_number = integer_part + fractional_part

print("Datatype of integer_part:", type(integer_part))
print("Datatype of fractional_part:", type(fractional_part))

print("Value of new_number:",new_number)
print("Datatype of new_number:",type(new_number))

Datatype of integer_part: <class 'str'>
Datatype of fractional_part: <class 'str'>
Value of new_number: 210.03
Datatype of new_number: <class 'str'>
